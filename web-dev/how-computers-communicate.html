How Computers Communicate

computers communicate similar to phones.

if i want to call you, i dial a number, your reciever rings, and if you pick up the phone, bang! we're connected.

computers do the same thing, but often instead of physical wires, they use a combination of wi-fi (wireless fidelity) and physical cables which are often copper (like a phone line) or fiber optic (which is like a glass tube with light bouncing around.

there are two common models to assist in understanding the mediums and protocols (which is a set of rules). the OSI model, and the TCP/IP models.
